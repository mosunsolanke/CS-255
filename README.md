CS-255 Project Two

Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?
Our client, DriverPass, is a small business that offers driving lesson services and seeks to automate its current system. DriverPass expects the new system to allow students to access online practice exams, book driving lessons, and enable their administrative team to view, schedule, edit driving reservations, as well as generate reports.
What did you do particularly well?
I think the preparation of the system provided to the client was executed exceptionally well. Creating plans and drafting a requirements document is vital for achieving a shared understanding of the system's functionality expectations. Keeping communication closed-loop guarantees that the client’s needs are addressed before we commence with the system design. Employing non-technical language is crucial in ensuring the client has a clear grasp of our objectives for the system, along with visuals that effectively illustrate the intended functionality of the system.
If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
If I had the opportunity to revise one aspect of my work from these documents, I would adjust my method for the UML Activity Diagrams. I think that when we showed these diagrams to our client, they should have been phrased in a more straightforward way to improve their understanding of the process being carried out.
How did you interpret the user's needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user's needs when designing?
I analyzed the user's requirements by dividing their expectations into smaller parts. It is crucial to grasp all of the client's criteria before proceeding with the design to ensure nothing is missed and all their expectations are fulfilled. For every element that the client wanted incorporated into the system, it was essential to capture that information in the requirements gathering document. This created a summary of the system's expectations and established a clear direction for the design.
How do you approach designing softare? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a system?
I believe the most effective way to design software should incorporate both top-down and bottom-up approaches. Top-down design allows the designer to develop a comprehensive understanding of how the final system should operate. This is then followed by breaking down individual functions to determine how each process should be executed to successfully create the system. Conversely, bottom-up design starts with the fundamental functionalities of each step and gradually builds up to the higher-level functions within the system. Both methods allow for an understanding of the system, from individual functions and intended uses to the overall framework when every component is fully operational. To design a system effectively, it should be done in stages. It is critical to gather project requirements and comprehend the outcomes the system must achieve before proceeding. After gathering the requirements, creating diagrams and outlines that illustrate both high-level and low-level functionalities of the system offers a more comprehensive design that highlights the relationships between actors and objects. Once the diagrams and outlines are finished, the design can be realized and meet the client's expectations.
